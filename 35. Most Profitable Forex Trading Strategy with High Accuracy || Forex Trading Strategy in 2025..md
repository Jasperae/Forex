### 3) Key concepts and terminology
- Breaker Block (breaker block concept)
  - A breaker block is a price block type used to identify where institutional activity may reverse or continue momentum.
  - The approach in this transcript emphasizes entering from the second breaker block, not the first.
  - Reason for second breaker block entry: reduces the risk of a spike up to your intended supply region before the entry, which could trigger a stop-loss or ruin the setup.
- SL and TP management
  - Stop loss placement is tied to a higher high (for sell entries) or a defined region near the breaker block.
  - Take profit targets (TP) are set according to the trader’s plan (e.g., TP1, TP2, or a calculated distance). The example mentions a TP target aligned with a 1.5 – 1.6 ratio in some setups.
- Structure of the setup
  - Look for a clear momentum direction (bearish in the discussed setup).
  - Use a breaker's block to determine where to enter and where to place risk controls (SL).
  - Some traders may enter near alternative points, but the standard approach here is to enter from the second breaker block with the SL above the recent high.

### 4) Trade setup and rationale (what to look for)
- Market context: identify bearish momentum and the presence of a trend line indicating supply/demand regions.
- Entry rule (breaker block approach):
  - Do not enter from the first breaker block.
  - Enter from the second breaker block region, where the price reacts and confirms the move with a suitable stop location and target.
- Stop loss placement:
  - SL is placed at a region above the higher high (for a sell) or at a predefined high around the breaker block, depending on the exact setup.
- Take profit:
  - Use predefined TP targets (e.g., TP1 around a measured distance or a multiple of risk).
  - Acknowledge that some trades may hit break-even or partial profits depending on momentum and market shocks (e.g., news events).
