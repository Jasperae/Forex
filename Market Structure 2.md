- Front: What is a demand zone?
  Back: A demand zone is a price area where buyers are interested in entering the market; it’s a zone for buying.

- Front: What is a supply zone?
  Back: A supply zone is a price area where sellers are interested in entering the market; it’s a zone for selling.

- Front: In what market condition is a demand zone typically found?
  Back: A demand zone is typically found in an uptrend.

- Front: In what market condition is a supply zone typically found?
  Back: A supply zone is typically found in a downtrend.

- Front: True or False: A demand zone is a single precise price.
  Back: False. A demand zone is a zone around a region, not necessarily a single price.

- Front: True or False: A supply zone can be identified by past market activity such as breaker blocks or other blocks.
  Back: True. Supply zones are often identified by past market activity like breaker blocks or other blocks.

- Front: What does a demand zone indicate about potential buyers?
  Back: It indicates a point of interest where many buyers may enter the market, often driving price up.

- Front: What does a supply zone indicate about potential sellers?
  Back: It indicates a point of interest where many sellers may enter the market, often driving price down.

- Front: How does a demand zone relate to future price movement (in relation to break of structure)?
  Back: The demand zone is where buyers push the price, potentially causing a break of structure from a previous level.

- Front: How is a supply zone related to break of structure?
  Back: The supply zone is where sellers cause price to move down, potentially leading to a break of structure in the opposite direction.

- Front: What is the relationship between trend and the location of demand/supply zones?
  Back: In an uptrend, you expect a demand zone; in a downtrend, you expect a supply zone.
