### Key Concepts to Understand (Recap)
- Inducement Model: A price action pattern where liquidity sits at a zone (blocks, gaps, highs/lows) and the market “induces” traders to place orders there before moving in the intended direction.
- Liquidity Concepts: Areas where stop orders or limit orders are likely to accumulate (e.g., around equal highs, equal lows, or specific blocks). The market often comes back to these zones to “clean” liquidity.
- Order Block: A price zone that acts as a reverse/impulsive move area and a potential location for entry when the price returns to it.
- Fair Value Gap (FVG): A price area where there was a void/inefficiency between candles; used as a potential pullback target or entry region.
- Adjustment Zone: A zone between blocks where liquidity concentration can shift; plays a role in taking or stopping out traders.
- Change of Character (CoC): A noticeable shift in market structure indicating potential trend reversal or continuation; used to time entries.
- Trend Context: Analysis starts with the higher time frame trend (e.g., downtrend or uptrend) and then looks for inducement-based entries on the chosen lower time frame (e.g., 1-hour).
- Risk Management: Patience is emphasized; not every potential zone yields an entry. Use precise entry triggers and consider stop loss placement around blocks, FVGs, and adjustment zones.

### Trading Framework Used in the Video
- Timeframe: Primary focus on the 1-hour chart; higher-timeframe context given for overall trend.
- Core Method: Inducement-based entries using liquidity zones, order blocks, and FVGs.
- Entry Triggers:
  - Buy Setup: Look for a bullish change of character, a valid bullish order block, and an inducement/adjustment zone that clears liquidity (e.g., a double-bottom, or a retracement to a known block/FVG with an eventual bullish reaction).
  - Sell Setup: Look for bearish change of character, a corresponding order block on the downside, or a liquidity-clearing move into a lower zone that triggers a sell continuation.
- Common Patterns Shown:
  - Price returns to a lower/higher block after a breakout, then produces a move in the direction implied by the inducement model.
  - Double bottom/double top analogies as signals for potential entry around blocks/adjustment zones.

- Execution Notes:

  - Other trades are short opportunities targeting the next liquidity level below.
  - Not all setups hit; the trader emphasizes backtesting across months (e.g., September through November) and learning from both wins and losses.
  - Partial take-profit strategies discussed (take part of the position at target and let the rest run) as a risk management technique.

### Step-by-Step Practice Process (From Transcript)
1) Set the context
   - Establish the overall market trend on a higher timeframe (e.g., downtrend in a given period).
   - Identify major liquidity blocks and potential order blocks on the 1-hour chart.
2) Identify a potential inducement zone
   - Mark areas where liquidity sits: equal highs/lows, observed blocks with liquidity-clearing candles.
   - Look for zones that could attract the price back (adjustment zones around blocks).
3) Look for a change of character
   - Wait for a clear CoC signal (price structure change indicating potential reversal or continuation).
4) Confirm a target zone
   - Use the next liquidity block or FVG as a target.
   - Consider the next major zone beyond the immediate block to set TP.
5) Execute with patience
   - If the setup is not clear or the inducement isn’t convincing, stay out and observe.
   - When entering, place risk management around the block/adjustment/FVG zone (tight stops near the candle that formed the block or the nearby liquidity zone).
6) Manage the trade
   - Use multi-entry concepts (take one part of the position at a logical TP and let the rest run if the momentum continues).
   - If price breaks above/below the inducement zone, reassess the positional risk and potential follow-ups.
7) Backtesting and learning
   - The presenter encourages backtesting across multiple months (e.g., September, October, November) to understand how inducement plays out in different market regimes.
   - Note both successful and failed entries to refine the model.

### Practical Examples Mentioned
- Buy example: A bullish move occurs after a break of structure, price retraces toward a lower block and an inducement zone, then a bullish entry is triggered with TP at the next liquidity level (or FVG/adjustment zone).
- Sell example: In a downtrend, price creates a supply block, tests a lower liquidity region, and a bearish move is triggered when the inducement clears liquidity and a new lower high/low is established.
- Adjusted zones: Sometimes the market forms an “adjustment” area that is not a direct block but still behaves as a liquidity magnet; this can be a practical entry target depending on the setup.

- Misinterpreting equal highs/lows as reliable liquidity without confirming a clearing candle.

### Glossary of Terms
- Inducement: A pattern where price action “induces” traders to place orders in liquidity zones before the price moves in the intended direction.
- Liquidity Zone: Regions where buy orders and sell orders are likely to cluster (blocks, equal highs/lows).
- Order Block: A price range where the market previously moved impulsively; a potential reversal or continuation zone.
- Fair Value Gap (FVG): A price area where the market left an inefficiency; a pullback target or entry area.
- Change of Character (CoC): A structural shift in price behavior suggesting a potential trend change.
- Adjustment Zone: A zone between blocks where liquidity is adjusted and can influence price action.
- TP (Take Profit): The price level at which profit is realized.
- AR Arrow/Trade Signal: A shorthand in the video describing risk-reward metrics or entries; part of the creator’s notation for trade quality.
