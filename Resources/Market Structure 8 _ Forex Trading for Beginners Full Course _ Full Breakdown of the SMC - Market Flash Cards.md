- Question: What is an uptrend in market structure?
  Answer: A pattern where the market moves upward, making higher highs and higher lows.

- Question: What is a downtrend in market structure?
  Answer: A pattern where the market moves downward, forming lower highs and lower lows.

- Question: What does the market do in a sideways or consolidating phase?
  Answer: The market moves in a range, not clearly trending up or down (up and down without a strong directional move).

- Question: What is a demand zone?
  Answer: The price area where buy orders are expected to come in, pushing the market upward.

- Question: What is a supply zone?
  Answer: The price area where sell orders are expected to come in, pushing the market downward.

- Question: In Market Structure, what is the main structure vs the sub-structure?
  Answer: The main structure is the higher timeframe trend (e.g., 1-hour). The sub-structure is the lower timeframe trend (e.g., 15 minutes, 5 minutes) that sits within the main structure.

- Question: What is a rally base rally pattern?
  Answer: A move up (rally), followed by a base (consolidation), then another rally upward.

- Question: What is a drop base drop pattern?
  Answer: A move down (drop), followed by a base (consolidation), then another drop downward.

- Question: What is a rally base drop pattern?
  Answer: A rally (move up), followed by a base, then a drop (move down).

- Question: What is a drop base rally pattern?
  Answer: A drop (move down), followed by a base, then a rally (move up).

- Question: How are premium and discount zones defined on a chart?
  Answer: Premium zone is the top part of a rectangle (where you would consider selling). Discount zone is the bottom part of the rectangle (where you would consider buying).

- Question: When do traders typically use premium and discount zones?
  Answer: Traders sell from the premium zone and buy from the discount zone; some use lower time frames to enter entries from the discount zone for precision.

- Question: What is a Break of Structure (BOS)?
  Answer: An impulsive move that breaks the current structural level, such as a higher high or a lower low, indicating a potential new direction.

- Question: What is Change of Character (ChoC)?
  Answer: A movement that changes the direction of the market trend (e.g., from up to down or from down to up).

- Question: What is a Shift of Market Structure (SMS)?
  Answer: When you observe weakening in the trend indicating a transition or shift in the market structure, often associated with a change of character.

- Question: How are Break of Structure, Change of Character, and SMS related?
  Answer: Break of Structure signals a new impulsive move breaking the current structure; Change of Character signals a directional change in trend; SMS indicates a weakening or shift in the prevailing structure, often occurring with a Change of Character.
