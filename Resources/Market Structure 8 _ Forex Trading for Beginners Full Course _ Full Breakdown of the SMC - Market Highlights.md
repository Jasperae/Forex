- Overview of Market Structure 8 (summary of the series)
- Trend types
  - Uptrend: price makes higher highs and higher lows (price moves up)
  - Downtrend: price makes lower highs and lower lows (price moves down)
  - Consolidation: price moves sideways (no clear up or down trend)
- Demand and Supply zones
  - Demand zone: where buy orders come in (drives price up)
  - Supply zone: where sell orders come in (drives price down)
- Main vs Sub structure
  - Main structure: the current higher time frame view (e.g., 1-hour)
  - Sub structure: lower time frame view (e.g., 15-minute, 5-minute)
- Rally-based and base patterns
  - Rally Base Rally (RBR)
  - Rally Based Drop (RBD)
  - Drop Base Rally (DBR)
  - Drop Base Drop (DBD)
  - Drop Based Valley (illustrated concept)
- Premium and discount levels
  - Use a rectangle to visualize zones
  - Premium zone (top of the rectangle): sell from the premium
  - Discount zone (bottom of the rectangle): buy from the discount
- Consolidation strategy
  - How to trade when the market is range-bound or unclear in direction
- Break of Structure (BOS)
  - An impulsive move that breaks the current structure (e.g., breaks a higher high or lower low)
- Change of Character (CoC)
  - A change in the direction of the trend (e.g., trend shift from up to down)
- Shift of Market Structure (SMS)
  - Observing weakness in a trend as structure begins to shift
- Connections between concepts
  - BOS and CoC are used to identify potential entries
  - CoC and SMS are related to shifts in trend strength and direction
