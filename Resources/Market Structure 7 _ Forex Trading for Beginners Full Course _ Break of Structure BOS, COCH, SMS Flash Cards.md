- Q: What is a break of structure (BOS) in an uptrend?
  A: A break of the previous high on the chart.

- Q: What is a break of structure (BOS) in a downtrend?
  A: A break of the previous low on the chart.

- Q: What do HH, HL, LH, and LL stand for on a price chart?
  A: HH = higher high, HL = higher low, LH = lower high, LL = lower low.

- Q: How is a break of structure identified on a chart?
  A: By marking the last high (in an uptrend) or last low (in a downtrend) and noting where price breaks beyond it.

- Q: What is change of character (CoC) in market structure?
  A: A move that changes the trend direction, from uptrend to downtrend or from downtrend to uptrend.

- Q: How is a change of character typically confirmed?
  A: After a break of market structure, followed by a retracement, then continuation in the new direction (often near a block or value area/POI).

- Q: What is shift of market structure (SMS)?
  A: A weakness or continuation indication that usually occurs before a change of character, signaling the market may shift direction.

- Q: Where is SMS usually seen in relation to a change of character?
  A: It often occurs before a change of character, as a sign the market is weakening and may reverse.

- Q: How would you describe an uptrend example in BOS terms?
  A: Price makes higher highs and higher lows; a BOS occurs when price breaks the last high, indicating a potential trend continuation or reversal depending on context.

- Q: How would you describe a downtrend example in BOS terms?
  A: Price makes lower lows and lower highs; a BOS occurs when price breaks the last low, indicating a potential trend continuation or reversal depending on context.

- Q: What is the practical way to mark a BOS on a chart?
  A: Use arrows to mark the break point at the previous high (uptrend) or previous low (downtrend) and label it BOS.

- Q: What is the practical meaning of a change of character after a BOS?
  A: It indicates the trend direction is changing, e.g., from up to down or from down to up, often with a retracement to a POI or liquidity area.

- Q: Can a change of character occur without a preceding clear BOS?
  A: It can, but a BOS often helps signal where the CoC might begin, as part of the transition.

- Q: What is the purpose of labeling BOS and CoC together in analysis?
  A: To identify where a trend may pause, reverse, or continue, aiding in entry/exit decisions.

- Q: What is the relationship between SMS and CoC?
  A: SMS indicates a shift in structure that often precedes or accompanies a change of character.

- Q: What should a trader pay attention to after a CoC occurs?
  A: A retracement to a block/value area or POI, which may provide a potential entry or liquidity target.

- Q: What is the recommended behavior when you see a shift of market structure?
  A: Recognize the potential shift toward a new trend and look for confirmation via a subsequent price move and retracement.

- Q: Why are these concepts important for profitability in trading?
  A: They help you identify structural changes in the market, providing edge in entry points and risk management.
