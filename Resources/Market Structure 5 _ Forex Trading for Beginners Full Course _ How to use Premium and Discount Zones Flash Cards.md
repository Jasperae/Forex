Q: What are premium and discount zones in forex trading?
A: They are two levels where you look to enter trades: premium is the upper (higher) zone, discount is the lower (lower) zone.

2)
Q: Which zone is called the premium zone?
A: The top/upper zone.

3)
Q: Which zone is called the discount zone?
A: The bottom/lower zone.

4)
Q: How is the price relationship described for premium vs discount?
A: Premium is the expensive price; discount is the cheaper price.

5)
Q: How can you identify the two zones on a chart?
A: Draw a rectangle (or use 50/50, Fibonacci, etc.) and use the top half as premium and the bottom half as discount.

6)
Q: In a buy setup, where would you enter the trade?
A: You buy in the discount zone and sell in the premium zone.

7)
Q: In a sell setup, where would you look to sell?
A: You sell in the premium zone (the higher price) and avoid selling in the discount zone.

8)
Q: What is the basic market structure idea behind premium and discount zones?
A: Buy low (discount) and sell high (premium) to capture price movement between the two zones.

9)
Q: What is the everyday-life analogy used to explain premium and discount?
A: Buying an iPhone at full price (premium) vs bargaining down to a discounted price (discount).

10)
Q: If you buy USD/another currency pair, where would a better entry price be?
A: A better entry price would be in the discount zone (lower price) before price moves to the premium zone.

11)
Q: Why would you prefer selling in the premium zone?
A: Selling in the premium zone means selling at a higher price, maximizing profit.

12)
Q: What kind of action should you take if the price is in the discount zone but trending toward the premium zone?
A: Be prepared to wait for the entry in the discount zone and then target the premium zone for exit.
