Flash Cards: Market Structure Concepts

1)
Front: What is a rally?
Back: An aggressive move of price to the upside.

2)
Front: What is a base?
Back: A consolidation or sideways movement in price (price moves up and down within a range).

3)
Front: What is a drop?
Back: An aggressive move of price to the downside.

4)
Front: What is a rally-based rally chart pattern?
Back: A sequence where a rally is followed by a base (consolidation) and then another rally.

5)
Front: What is a rally-based drop chart pattern?
Back: A sequence where a rally is followed by a base and then a drop (the price moves down after the base).

6)
Front: What is a drop-based drop chart pattern?
Back: A sequence where a drop is followed by a base (consolidation) and then another drop.

7)
Front: What is a drop-based rally chart pattern?
Back: A sequence where a drop is followed by a base and then a rally.

8)
Front: How does a base typically look on a chart?
Back: Price moves sideways within a range, creating a consolidation area (up-down or sideways).

9)
Front: How is a rally visually identified on a chart?
Back: A strong, aggressive move upward in price, often with clear momentum.

10)
Front: How is a drop visually identified on a chart?
Back: A strong, aggressive move downward in price, often with clear momentum.

11)
Front: What does “R” stand for in the examples?
Back: Rally.

12)

Back: Base (consolidation).
